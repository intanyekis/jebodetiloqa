Early Life and Career

Steven Bauer, born on August 7, 1956, in Havana, Cuba, immigrated to the United States with his family following the Cuban Revolution. Coming from a Cuban background, Bauer’s journey to Hollywood began in Miami, Florida. He attended Miami Dade High School and later pursued his passion for acting at Miami Dade Community College and the University of Miami.

Bauer’s early career consisted of various roles in theater and television before his breakthrough role in the iconic 1983 film “Scarface”. In the movie, Bauer portrayed the character Manny Ribera, a trusted friend of Tony Montana, played by Al Pacino. His exceptional performance in “Scarface” established him as a talented actor and opened doors for further opportunities in the industry.

Throughout his acting journey, Steven Bauer has showcased his versatility and talent, captivating audiences with his captivating performances. Steven Bauer’s Personal Life

In addition to his successful acting career, Steven Bauer has had a colorful personal life. He has been married four times and has children from his previous marriages.

Bauer’s first marriage was to Melanie Griffith, whom he met while filming “She’s in the Army Now.” The couple tied the knot in 1981 but unfortunately divorced in 1989.

After his separation from Griffith, Bauer went on to marry his second wife, Ingrid Anderson, in 1989. However, their marriage ended in divorce in the same year.

Despite the challenges in his personal life, Bauer found love again and married his third wife, Christiana Boney, in 1992. Unfortunately, their relationship also ended in divorce in 2002.

Finally, in 2003, Bauer found lasting love and stability with his fourth and current wife, Paulette Miltimore. They have been together for over 18 years now.

While Bauer’s personal life has experienced ups and downs, his commitment to love and family reflects his determined nature both on and off the screen.
