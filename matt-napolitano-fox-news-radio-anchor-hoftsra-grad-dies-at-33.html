Matt Napolitano: Fox News radio anchor &amp; Hoftsra grad dies at 33

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, June 7, 2024

Remembering Matt Napolitano: A Fox News Radio Anchor and Resilient Soul

On December 23, 2023, the world of journalism mourned the loss of Matt Napolitano, a beloved Fox News Radio anchor and distinguished graduate of Hofstra University.

At the tender age of 33, Napolitano succumbed to complications from lupus, leaving behind a legacy of impactful storytelling and unwavering resilience.

Early Life and Education

Born and raised in Franklin Square, Matt Napolitano discovered his passion for broadcast journalism early on. He pursued his dreams at Hofstra University, where he honed his skills and developed a deep understanding of the media landscape.

Career Beginnings

Napolitano’s journey in the field of journalism began in 2015 when he joined the Fox News team.

However, his dedication to storytelling and his captivating voice had already garnered attention, leading him to work with notable media outlets such as Merlin Media and TMZ before finding his home at Fox News.

Throughout his career, he became renowned for his work on sports stories and his distinctive contributions to various Fox programs.

A Bright Personality and Lasting Connections

Matt Napolitano was more than a talented journalist—he was known for his bright personality and the ease with which he connected with others in the New York media scene.

Colleagues and friends alike remember him as someone who brought energy, enthusiasm, and a genuine love for his craft to every project.

Living with Lupus

Napolitano’s battle with lupus was a testament to his resilience and strength. For nearly two decades, he faced the challenges of living with an autoimmune disease while continuing to excel in his career.
