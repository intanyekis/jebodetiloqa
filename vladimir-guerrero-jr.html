    Vladimir Guerrero Jr. Facts

  • A Canadian citizen, Vladimir Guerrero Jr. was born in Montreal while his father was playing for the Montreal Expos.
  • At a young age, he shifted to the Dominican Republic along with his mother and spent most of his early childhood there.
  • He was very young when his parents separated and his mother subsequently relocated to Santiago, Dominican Republic. He grew up splitting his time between Santiago with his mother, Don Gregorio with his uncle Wilton, and in the United States with his father during the summer.
  • Vladimir Guerrero Jr. was ranked as the top international free agent by Baseball America when he was eligible for free agency in 2015 and was ranked as the fourth-best by MLB.com.
  • He was 16 years old when he signed a $3.9 million deal with the Toronto Blue Jays on July 2, 2015.
  • Vladimir Guerrero Jr. has used the English language to speak with his Blue Jays teammates and team staff but has given media interviews, including the one on the field after winning the 2021 All-Star Game MVP, in Spanish with the help of the team’s interpreter. In 2019, he stated, “I’m trying to learn as quickly as possible so I can talk to fans.”
