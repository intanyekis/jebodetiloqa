Joe Ingram Car Accident, Memphis Tennessee, Church Of God In Christ Member has died

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, June 21, 2024
0SHARESShareTweet

Joe Ingram Obituary, Death – On Wednesday, July 12, 2023, at UT Medical Center, Jesus appeared to Joe’s bedside and took him to paradise. Joe’s mother and second father were present at the time. Joe attended and graduated from Sequoyah High School, which is located in Madisonville, Tennessee. His father, Joe F. Ingram Jr., both sets of grandparents, two uncles and two aunts, and two other relatives all passed away before him.

Brother, Russ Ingram; Half-sister, Zoe Ingram; Nieces, Aliya Ingram & Charlie Hatcher, Emma Ingram; Half-Nephew T.J. ONeil; 4 aunts; and many, many cousins; Special Friends, Teona Williams Cooper and Josh Scarborough. Survived by Mother, Mildred Hanley Green, and second Dad, Bob Green. Also survived by Special Friends, Teona Williams Cooper and Josh Scarborough.

They were friends all the way through elementary school, all the way through high school, and they continue to be friends now. Sunsets in the Smoky Mountains, trips to the beach, and the ocean were some of Joe’s favorite things. He was a passionate Vol for life fan and attended many of the games throughout his lifetime. He attended a lot of matches and got to know a lot of wrestlers because to his interest in the sport.

When he went to see Shenandoah in concert, the lead singer, Joe, and he had numerous conversations about football. Country music was his favorite genre of music, and his favorite country band was Shenandoah. Joe had a lot of love for our three fur babies, Romeo, Juliette, and Stitch, and our fur babies had the same amount of love for their human brother, Joe.

They remember how he would always pitch in to help take care of them, and they mourn having their brother around. On Thursday, July 20, 2023, the family will have a reception at Smith Trinity Chapel beginning at 5:00 PM and continuing until 7:00 PM. The Funeral Service will immediately follow at 7:00 PM, and Reverend Mike Blair will preside over the proceedings.
