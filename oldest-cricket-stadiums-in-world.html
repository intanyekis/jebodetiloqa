Top 10 Oldest Cricket Stadiums in the World

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, August 12, 2024

Do you know that England and Australia played the first international cricket match in 1877? This match took place at the Melbourne Cricket Ground. Cricket is one of the most popular and appreciated games in the world. The popularity of the game increases with the increased number of cricket grounds.

In this article, we have listed the Top 10 Oldest Cricket Stadiums in the World:

10 Oldest Cricket Stadium in the World

1. Himley Cricket Club Ground

  • Year of Establishment: 1845
  • Address: Cricket Club Office, Stourbridge Rd., Himley, Dudley, DY3 4LB, United Kingdom

Himley Cricket Club Ground is the oldest cricket stadium in the world. It was founded in 1883. This stadium is now available with many facilities, like two cricket squares at the Stourbridge Road facility, top junior sections in the country, etc. 

2. Lord’s Cricket Ground (The Home of Cricket)

  • Year of Establishment: 1814 (established in 1787)
  • Address: St John’s Wood Rd., St John’s Wood, London, NW8 8QN, United Kingdom.

Lord’s Cricket Ground (The Home of Cricket) is one of the oldest cricket stadiums in the world. It was established in 1787. As per its Wikipedia page, the first match played was White Conduit Club vs. Middlesex on Monday, May 21, 1787.

3. Kennington Oval (The Oval)

  • Year of Establishment: 1845
  • Address: Kennington, London SE11 5SS, United Kingdom

Kennington Oval (The Oval) is named Kia Oval. It is an international cricket ground in Kennington, located in the borough of Lambeth, in south London. It was the first ground in England to host international Test cricket in September 1880. 

4. M. Chinnaswamy Stadium

  • Year of Establishment: 1849
  • Address: MG Road, Near Gandhi Park, Ambedkar Veedhi, Shivaji Nagar, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560001, India.

The M. Chinnaswamy Stadium is one of the world’s oldest cricket stadiums. It is located in Bangalore, Karnataka, India. It is owned by the government of Karnataka. It is the first cricket stadium in the world to have solar panels. It was under the “Go Green” initiative of the KSCA

5. Old Trafford

  • Year of Establishment: 1857
  • Address: Talbot Rd, Old Trafford, Stretford, Manchester M16 0PX, United Kingdom.

Old Trafford comes under one of the oldest cricket stadiums in the world. It is located in  Old Trafford, Greater Manchester, England. This stadium offers a seating capacity of 74,310. It is the  largest club football stadium in the United Kingdom. It was also termed as “The Theatre of Dreams” by Bobby Charlton. 

6. Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG)

  • Year of Establishment: 1853
  • Address: Brunton Ave, Richmond VIC 3002, Australia.

Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) is also counted as the world’s oldest cricket stadium. It is an Australian sports stadium located in Yarra Park, Melbourne, Victoria. It is managed by Melbourne Cricket Club. This stadium is the largest stadium in the Southern Hemisphere. It is the second-largest cricket ground by capacity, after the Narendra Modi Stadium in India. This stadium seats 95,000 with an additional 5,000 standing room capacity.

7. Eden Gardens

  • Year of Establishment: 1864
  • Address: BBD Bagh, Kolkata, West Bengal, 700021, India

The Eden Gardens is the world’s oldest cricket stadium. It is located in Kolkata, India. It was located in 1864. It is the oldest cricket stadium. The Bengal cricket team and the Kolkata Knight Riders play their home matches at this stadium. This stadium has hosted many T20I and ODI matches. This stadium has a seating capacity of 68,000. 

8. Sydney Cricket Ground (SCG)

  • Year of Establishment: 1848
  • Address: Driver Ave, Moore Park, Sydney, NSW 2021, Australia

The Sydney Cricket Ground (SCG) is also listed as one of the old Melbourne cricket grounds. This stadium is located at Moore Park suburb of Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. This stadium has hosted many matches, like Test, One Day International, and Twenty20 cricket. It is the home ground for the New South Wales Blues cricket team. This stadium is managed by Venues NSW, an agency of the Government of New South Wales

9. The Grange Club

  • Year of Establishment: 1832
  • Address: Portgower Pl., Edinburgh, EH4 1HQ, United Kingdom.

The Grange Club is counted as one of the oldest cricket stadiums in the world. It is located in the Stockbridge district of Edinburgh, Scotland. This ground is also known as the Grange. This stadium has hosted Scotland’s first official One Day International (ODI). It was also selected to host the 2015 ICC World Twenty20 Qualifier tournament.

10. Chesterfield

  • Year of Establishment: 1863
  • Address: Queen’s Park, Boythorpe Rd., Chesterfield, S40 2BE, United Kingdom/

Chesterfield Stadium, established in 1863, stands as one of the world’s oldest cricket venues. Located in Queen’s Park, Chesterfield, UK, it has witnessed over a century of cricket history. This historic ground continues to uphold the traditions of the sport, preserving its rich heritage for future generations.
