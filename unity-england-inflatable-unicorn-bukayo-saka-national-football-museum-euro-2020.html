Unity, the England inflatable unicorn made famous by Bukayo Saka, gets permanent home at National Fo

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, July 19, 2024

Bukayo Saka's Euro 2020 campaign will be remembered for all the right reasons in the National Football Museum.

His inflatable unicorn, Unity, will be now be showcased as an important item of memorabilia in Manchester.

While Saka failed to convert the crucial spot-kick which gave Italy victory in the final at Wembley, he was at the centre of an inspiring young squad which galvanised a nation.

Not only did they thrive on the pitch, Gareth Southgate's men were a joy to watch off it, too.

England squads of yesteryear didn't get on all that well, but this team put domestic colours aside and developed an incredible sense of togetherness.

That much was clear during their down time between matches, most of which was spent on inflatables in the pool.

It was Saka's antics which became iconic, with the Arsenal teenager pictured riding and falling from an inflatable unicorn.

Now, as part of a Euros Heroes exhibition, the floating animal, suitably named Unity, will be visited by adoring fans this weekend.
