Who is Molly Rose? Biography and Wiki

Molly Rose is an American journalist who is widely recognized for working at KTVI FOX 2 News in St.Louis, Missouris as a Morning Traffic anchor and reporter. She joined the station after previously working at WDAF in Kansas City where she worked for three good years. Molly also worked as a columnist for WVIR in Charlottesville, Virginia. There she was recognized by the Virginia Associated Press for her incredible work.

Molly lived in Dellwood until second grade and attended Griffith Elementary School. She grew up just across the river from St. Louis, in Edwardsville, Illinois, and graduated from Edwardsville High School. After High School Rose attended the University of Missouri School of Journalism. One of her favorite places in St. Louis is Forest Park. Rose loves her St. Louis Cardinals and is a huge animal lover, spending much of her time outside with her 7-year-old black lab rescue dog Avery.

    Molly Rose Career

    She started her journalism career more than a decade ago when she served as editor-in-chief for her high school newspaper. On November 30, 2017, KTVI/FOX 2 News, Tribune Broadcasting’s St. Louis FOX affiliate, welcomed her back home to St. Louis, where she is now serving as a reporter, weeknights.

    Rose joined KTVI from WDAF, Tribune Broadcasting’s television station in Kansas City, where she spent three years as a reporter. Before that, she worked as an anchor/reporter at WVIR (NBC) in Charlottesville, Virginia. While she worked for WVIR she was recognized by the Virginia Associated Press for her work.

    Molly appreciates that journalism allows her to investigate wrongdoings. She received the 2014 National Edward R. Murrow Award for one of her investigations revealing an incident in which the University of Virginia Hospital mistakenly sent a family home with their deceased loved ones amputated body parts. Rose has also been recognized by the Virginia Associated Press for her great work.
